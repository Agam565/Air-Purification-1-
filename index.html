Air Purification

When two or more substances are combined but don’t react chemically, they form a mixture. Substances in a mixture can be separated by physical means, such as filtration. Different mixtures require different methods of separation. Sometimes, several methods of separation are needed to isolate or separate a substance.

All air filters use the theory of filtration.

Filtration - Liquid particles can pass through the filter and be collected, while the solid particles are held back. E.g. A mixture of an insoluble solid and liquid, such as sand in water can be separated by filtration.

Process of an Air Filter

  • Air passes through the filter screen.
  • As the air passes through, the filter material catches particulate matter such as pollen, dust, pet dander, dirt and allergens.
  • Every second as air passes through the filter, the contaminants build up.
  • Eventually, the filter gets too clogged, airflow is reduced, air filter is then replaced and the process continues.

*Note

The quality of the filter materials(media)influences the size of air particles that can be captured.
